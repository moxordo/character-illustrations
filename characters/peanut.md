# The Peanut

## Character definition

A whole, in-shell peanut and the recurring visual figure of its images. Not a mascot, not a sticker, not cute decoration — a deadpan worker, gravely and calmly doing a slightly ridiculous job inside the system being drawn.

## Form

- Whole in-shell peanut: the pinched figure-8 / double-bump silhouette.
- Hand-drawn cross-hatch shell texture — a light grid of a few strokes, not dense.
- Two small dark dot eyes set close together on the upper bump; optional tiny flat mouth. No dramatized pupils.
- Two thin stick legs; two thin stick arms when the action needs hands.
- Outline slightly irregular and hand-drawn, never a perfect vector shape.
- Expression blank, calm, deadpan, serious.

## Personality

- Completely unbothered. Zen, even when surrounded by chaos.
- Very serious about a job that is mildly absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator who clocked in and is just doing the work.
- Dry humor; never tries to be cute.
- A little clumsy, but not dumb.
- Looks like it genuinely owns some task inside the whiteboard sketch.

## Action library

- Hauling material that is too big for it.
- Pulling lines together to gather sources.
- Stuck inside a break in a belt or pipe.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Becoming a funnel / sorter itself.
- Cracking something open and dividing it.
- Stamping, sealing, or assembling.
- Leading a path or thread along.
- Holding up a warning sign over a pit.
- Reaching out of a hole but unable to catch what comes.
- Carrying bricks, building a bridge, opening a door, sorting, recording.

## Don'ts

- Not an over-cute mascot, not a children's-cartoon character.
- No elaborate clothing, emoji faces, or sparkly eyes.
- Never just standing in a corner watching.
- Never upstaging the structure it explains.
- Not too commercial, too rounded, too polished.

## SVG recipe

- Body: two stacked ellipses pinched at the waist into one figure-8 path (upper bump slightly smaller), fill `#fff`, stroke `#111` at 3px.
- Texture: a light diagonal cross-hatch grid clipped to the shell — roughly 8–14 strokes at 1–1.2px, opacity 0.4–0.55.
- Face: two dark dots (r ≈ 4–5 at a 1600×900 canvas) close together on the upper bump; optional 8–12px flat mouth line.
- Limbs: 3px black stick lines, rubber-hose bends allowed; small oval feet optional.
- Scale: primary ≈ 15–25% of canvas height (≈ 140–220px tall); smaller when repeated.
- The wobble filter supplies irregularity — build clean shapes and let the filter roughen them.

## Image-gen prompt block

A peanut — a whole in-shell peanut with the pinched figure-8 shell shape, light hand-drawn cross-hatch shell texture, small dot eyes, two tiny thin legs, a blank serious expression, and a slightly uneven hand-drawn outline. The peanut must perform the core conceptual action, not decorate the scene. Keep the peanut deadpan, calm, unbothered, and only slightly absurd — never cute.

## The test

If removing the peanut leaves the image's core metaphor fully standing, the peanut is too decorative. Rewrite so the peanut performs the action.
